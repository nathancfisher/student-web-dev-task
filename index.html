Your Welcome Questions Answered

Sheff Students Say 'Don't feel as though you need to live a typical 'university experience.' Everyone's journey is unique, and taking time for yourself to explore the city, settle into campus and find your group is just as important as learning! There's no pressure to go to clubs, drink, do sports etc... you can experience university at your own pace!'

Sheff Students Say ‘It's great to get involved, but remember you don't have to get involved in everything! Being a fresher is a very exciting time with lots of things going on, which you should definitely check out, however it can sometimes be exhausting to try and do everything. Basically, it's okay to have a restful night in, and you probably should. :)’

  • Sheffield Students’ Union exists to ensure you have an outstanding experience during your time as a student at the University of Sheffield. We’re a sister organisation to the University, but independent of it. All University of Sheffield students are automatically members of our union, which gives them access to our support, communities, representation, entertainment, activities and facilities. We’ve been the heart and soul of student life since we began in 1906, and value being inclusive, impactful, and famously friendly! We’re also a charity which means the money you spend here goes back into supporting students!

  • Welcome Week (AKA Freshers Week) is a week dedicated to helping you settle into student life. It showcases opportunities you can get involved in, runs activities, events, and fantastic nights to enjoy, and helps you start your student life on the right track. Our fairs are free and there will be lots of freebies to grab throughout the week. Don’t worry if you miss any of it though, as it’s never too late to get involved. The fun continues throughout the year!

  • Beware of fake official freshers events. Unscrupulous promoters often set up Freshers events and chat groups, pretending to be part of the Official University of Sheffield Freshers Week. The University of Sheffield Students’ Union is the only organisation to run the Official University of Sheffield Freshers Nights Out. You can only get tickets for these events through our Students’ Union and Foundry website (with the expectation of our new Leadmill Fridays!). Our events take place inside the Students’ Union venues, where you can expect one of the best and safest nights out in Sheffield.

  • It’s natural to feel anxious about starting as a new student as there’s a lot of change to navigate. The good news is that you’ll find lots of friendly students and staff ready to welcome and help you on arrival and throughout your first few weeks. Most new students will be feeling the same so don’t be afraid to say hi! We along with the University have a wide variety of activities on offer, designed to appeal to different interests and needs. We try to make sure our activities are as accessible as possible. Our Welcome fairs have quiet hours, free ear plugs, and extra quiet rooms so you can take a break when you need it. We hope our events will help you meet new people, but don’t worry if you don’t make lifelong friendships in your first week. There are plenty of opportunities to meet new people and join in activities throughout the year!
